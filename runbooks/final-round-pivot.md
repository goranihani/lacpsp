# Final-Round Pivot

Use only if selected for the final round. Keep the strategy simple: do not invent a new system overnight.

## After Main-Round Results

1. Record only general process lessons.
2. Do not publish or share the main-round problem, answer, or AI outputs.
3. Keep the same Track A / Track B split.
4. Pick three improvements:
   - one legal accuracy improvement
   - one AI-usage report improvement
   - one submission safety improvement

## Final-Round Differences

| Main Round | Final Round |
| --- | --- |
| assigned problem may differ by team | common problem for finalists |
| problem-specific ranking | direct differentiation against top teams |
| issue coverage is key | judgment quality and distinctive practical insight matter more |

## Final-Round Differentiators

Choose at most three:

- clearer direct conclusions
- better counterargument handling
- narrower but verified foreign-law use
- stronger AI limitation and practical remediation section
- cleaner source status ledger
- faster last-15-minute submission lock

## Overnight Rule

No new grand system. Print, rehearse, sleep.
